A dataset that you manipulated or queried using Python

I used the Trump Twitter Archive to analyze the words Donald Trump uses in his tweets over time. The data comes from the website: http://www.trumptwitterarchive.com/ which has been compiled by Brendan Brown through a Twitter scraping code of his own creation. The tweets update into a JSON on github (linked above and also found in the "faq" section of the archive's website) which is what I used to do my analysis.

How did your manipulation of the data change how an audience might interpret it?

Trump's tweets on their own are overwhelming in number (tens of thousands of tweets over ten+ years) so I wanted to make something that would analyze his language usage over the decade that he's been on Twitter and make it easy for people to see the changes that occurred. It's difficult to analyze someone's entire Twitter when it is as enormous as Trump's so my taking the data and turning it into word clouds by year broke that language down in a way that is understandable and comprehensive. If I had not manipulated the data in this way, it would be hard for an audience to interprete the meanings and patterns among Trump's word choices over time because there is such a huge volume of data and the JSON is difficult to read on its own. By putting it into Python though, I was able to break it down so that I could visualize the words used and make my findings more accessible to a general audience.
